# Timeline de cambios 2024-2026 y features retiradas

> Parte del skill **linkedin-profile-optimizer**. Derivado del knowledge base (pack `tendencias-2026`). Consultar cuando el usuario pregunte 'que ha cambiado' o para NO recomendar tacticas/features obsoletas.

---

## Resumen

Between 2024 and 2026 LinkedIn rebuilt its core mechanics. Creator Mode was retired (Feb-Mar 2024) and its tools (Follow CTA, newsletters, analytics, Featured) were democratized to all profiles; profile hashtags were removed. Thought Leader Ads expanded (Apr 1 2024) to let brands sponsor ANY member's content (not just employees), plus articles/newsletters. A dedicated short-form vertical video feed/tab launched in 2024 (TikTok-style, +36% video viewership). The single biggest shift is the AI ranking migration: 360Brew, a 150B-parameter decoder-only foundation model (arXiv 2501.16450, Jan 27 2025), replaced fragmented ranking systems and rolled into the feed through 2025-early 2026, moving LinkedIn from a Relationship Graph to an Interest Graph (only ~31% of the average feed now comes from 1st-degree connections). Richard van der Blom's Algorithm Insights 2026 (1.3M posts) documents a hard reach reset: views ~-50%, engagement ~-25%, follower growth ~-59% YoY, with reach down ~60% for active creators over two years. The algorithm now rewards dwell time, topic authority, comments and meaningful engagement over likes/hashtags, and actively penalizes external links (~18.8% median reach hit for one in-body link per van der Blom; the first-comment workaround is also now penalized) and unedited generic AI output. Document/PDF carousels (~6.6%) and native vertical video lead engagement; text-only lags (~2%). Skills-based hiring accelerated with AI skill verification, and the creator economy matured (450k+ verified creators, monetization programs).

| Interest Graph shift | Optimize for the Interest Graph, not your existing network — strangers see your content based on topical relevance | LinkedIn moved from Relationship Graph (people you know) to Interest Graph (what you engage with), so niche relevance beats follower count. | Pick a narrow, consistent topic so 360Brew can classify you cleanly (semantic clarity). Reach beyond your network depends on topic consistency + engagement quality. |

| Dwell time | Engineer for dwell time (read time), which now outranks reactions | 360Brew measures time-spent engaging as a top relevance signal; bounce behavior is read as low quality. | Use strong 1-2 line hooks above the 'see more' fold, line breaks, and longer-form value so people stop and read. A post read 30 seconds beats 50 quick likes. Avoid 'click bounces' (immediate exits), which get the post deprioritized. |

Post when your audience is active, reply to every early comment, and prompt discussion to win the test. |
| External links | Keep links out of the post body; even the first-comment workaround is now penalized | LinkedIn suppresses off-platform links to keep users in-feed; native content is favored. | Per van der Blom 2026, one in-body external link cuts median reach ~18.8% (some practitioner data cites up to ~60% for link-heavy posts). Put the value natively in the post and add the link only after engagement lands, or via DM/'comment for link', knowing the comment trick is now also dampened. |
| Posting frequency | Aim for 2-4 high-quality posts per week on personal profiles; do not post daily by default | Quality and topic consistency beat volume under the Interest Graph; oversaturation cannibalizes your own reach. | Moving from 1 to 2-4 posts/week adds ~1,234 impressions per post (van der Blom). Daily posting shows ~-26% average reach per post and ~-45% reach over time from content fatigue. Company Pages: 3-5 posts/week. |
| Format: documents/carousels | Prioritize PDF document carousels — the highest-engagement format | Documents maximize dwell time (swiping) which 360Brew rewards. | ~6.6% engagement. | Format: native vertical video | Upload native vertical video (9:16) 30-90 seconds to ride the dedicated video feed | LinkedIn surfaces native vertical video in a separate TikTok-style feed plus the main feed — double distribution. | ~5.6% engagement; native video gets a distribution boost via the video tab launched in 2024 (+36% platform video viewership). Add captions (≈91% watch muted), 1:1 or 9:16, native upload (never YouTube/external embeds). |
| Format hierarchy | Use the engagement hierarchy: documents (~6.6%) > native video (~5.6%) > image+text (~3.2%) > text-only (~2.0%) | Format choice now materially changes baseline reach under 360Brew. | Default to documents and video for reach-critical posts; reserve text-only for fast takes and conversation starters where comment velocity matters. |
| Comments over likes | Design posts to earn comments and replies, not likes | Meaningful conversation is a primary 360Brew quality signal; likes are low-value. | End with a specific question, reply to commenters with substance (which itself counts as engagement), and seed discussion. Comments and dwell weigh far more than reactions. |
| Hashtags | Use 0-3 relevant hashtags max; never spam | 360Brew reads topic from content; excessive hashtags look spammy and trigger suppression. | Posts without hashtags can outperform hashtagged ones by 5-10%; 10+ hashtags risk a 30-50% visibility penalty. If used, keep to 3-5 highly specific tags. Hashtags are now a minor/weak signal vs semantic topic classification. |
| AI content detection | Never publish unedited generic AI output — it gets flagged and throttled | The platform actively de-ranks low-effort AI to fight feed saturation; human signal is the differentiator. | LinkedIn's NLP classifiers flag generic openers, bullet-heavy structures with no personal voice, and templated frameworks repeated across accounts; flagged low-effort AI posts get sharply reduced initial distribution. Four specific over-used templated phrases each pull ~4-7% below normal reach in 2026. Use AI to draft, then add personal voice, specifics, and lived experience. |
| Newsletters | Build a LinkedIn Newsletter to bypass feed-algorithm risk | Newsletters give algorithm-independent reach — a hedge against the reach reset. | Newsletter editions are pushed to subscribers via notification + email regardless of feed ranking. Convert engaged followers to subscribers; publish on a consistent cadence. Available to all profiles since Creator Mode democratization. |
| Thought Leader Ads (Pages) | For company pages, use Thought Leader Ads to sponsor employee AND non-employee member content | Member-voiced sponsored content outperforms brand-voiced ads on trust and engagement. | Since Apr 1 2024 brands can sponsor any member's post (with consent), including customers/partners, plus articles and newsletters. Find by name (1st/2nd degree) or post URL in Campaign Manager; ad ties to the parent Page. ~73% of decision-makers trust thought-leadership over marketing materials. |
| Skills-based hiring | Lead profiles and Pages with demonstrated skills, not just credentials | Employers and AI matching weight demonstrated, verified skills over academic credentials. | Add and get verified on the 2026 'Skills on the Rise' (AI implementation, prompt engineering, plus human skills: executive communication, leadership influence, cross-functional collaboration). Pursue verification, collaborative posts (rolling out from Cannes 2025), and co-hosted Lives to tap others' networks. |

## 2024->2026 change audit (what to update in any old LinkedIn advice)

- [ ] Remove all 'enable Creator Mode' instructions (retired 2024)
- [ ] Remove 'add hashtags to your profile' (removed 2024)
- [ ] Reframe reach expectations to the post-reset baseline (~-50% views)
- [ ] Replace 'hashtags drive reach' with 'topic consistency drives reach' (360Brew/Interest Graph)
- [ ] Move links out of post body and drop first-comment link trick
- [ ] Switch default cadence from daily to 2-4 posts/week
- [ ] Add AI-editing step so no raw/generic AI text ships
- [ ] Add dwell-time hook + format upgrade (documents/native video)

## Format & distribution checklist per post

- [ ] Pick highest-fit high-engagement format (document > native video > image+text > text)
- [ ] Hook in first 1-2 lines above the fold
- [ ] No external link in body
- [ ] 0-3 specific hashtags max
- [ ] Post Tue-Thu 8-10 AM audience time
- [ ] Reply to every comment in the first 60 minutes
- [ ] No edits during golden hour
- [ ] On-topic for your authority lane

## Tacticas/features obsoletas — NO recomendar

- Telling users to 'turn on Creator Mode' — it was retired in 2024; the features are now default, accessed differently.
- Adding 'hashtags I talk about' to profiles — removed Feb 2024 and irrelevant to 360Brew's semantic ranking.
- Assuming hashtags drive reach — they're now a weak signal; 10+ triggers a 30-50% penalty and no-hashtag posts often win.
- Putting external links in the post body or relying on the first-comment link trick — both are now penalized.
- Posting daily 'for the algorithm' — daily cadence reduces per-post reach ~26% and ~45% over time vs 2-4/week.
- Publishing unedited generic AI output — flagged by NLP classifiers and throttled; templated phrases cost 4-7% reach each.
- Optimizing for likes — comments and dwell time matter far more under 360Brew.
- Editing posts during the golden hour — can reset/dampen distribution testing.
- Believing reach drops are a personal failure — there was a platform-wide structural reset (~-50% views); benchmark against the new baseline.
- Treating Company Pages as the primary organic engine — personal profiles outreach Pages; Pages need employee advocacy + paid.
- Topic-hopping — fragments semantic classification and kills the topic-authority signal.
- Embedding YouTube/external video instead of native uploads — loses the vertical-video-feed distribution boost.
- Myth that 'dwell time can be faked with long posts' — click-bounce detection deprioritizes posts people open and immediately leave.
